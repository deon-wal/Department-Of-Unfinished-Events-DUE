The Nervous System

The Archive Floor is not a library; it is a biological entity. Monolithic, 50-foot-tall filing cabinets stretch upward, disappearing entirely into the Memory Mist.

Every file housed here is a "narrative thread" that actively sustains reality. Autonomous rolling ladders transit the aisles with independent intent, tracing the pathways of the World's Nervous System.

The Medium

Active Vellum

Class: Organic

Files do not merely store information; they experience it. The vellum exhibits a rhythmic, heart-like pulse, causing a low-frequency hum to resonate through the floorboards.

A papercut from Active Vellum does not draw blood; it draws your earliest childhood memory. Ten papercuts result in complete ego-death.

Under extreme emotional charge, the files become physically hot to the touch. When narrative tension peaks, the vellum begins to actively sweat blood-ink off the page, staining the hands of the Archivist.

Identified Hazards

Memory Parasites

Entity: Silver Moths

Swarms that actively devour archived memories from the vellum. A single bite to an Agent results in immediate loss of first names, childhood color associations, and facial recognition logic.

Critical Threat

Narrative Combustion

Slang: "The Burn Out"

When a story or life terminates abruptly or unexpectedly, the corresponding file undergoes File Collapse. The result is immediate, explosive incineration of the vellum into floating ash.
